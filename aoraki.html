Aoraki/Mount Cook National Park

Aoraki/Mount Cook National Park located within the Mackenzie Region. It is a majestic landscape which are dominated by the Southern Alps and has the highest peak in New Zealand. Aoraki/Mount Cook stands at a towering 3,274 meters (12,218 feet) which makes it a beacon for mountaineers from around the globe

Best Time to Visit: Summer (Nov–Mar) for hiking, Winter offers skiing.

  • Fees: No entry fee for the national park.
  • Accommodation: Limited in Mt Cook Village (e.g., The Hermitage Hotel), plus DOC campsites/huts.
  • Key Activities: Hiking (Hooker Valley Track, Mueller Hut), glacier boat tours, scenic flights, and stargazing.
