Abel Tasman National Park

Abel Tasman National Park, New Zealand's smallest national park about 22,530 hectares which is located at the top of the South Island. It is famous for its golden-sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and coastal forests.

Best Time to Visit: September to April, with summer (Dec-Feb) being busiest, while shoulder seasons offer quieter, scenic experiences.

  • Access: Mostly via water taxi, boat, or kayak from Marahau or Kaiteriteri.
  • Top Activities: Sea kayaking, walking the Abel Tasman Coast Track (a Great Walk), swimming, and camping
  • Must-See Spots: Split Apple Rock, Cleopatra’s Pool, and various secluded bays like Anchorage and Awaroa.
